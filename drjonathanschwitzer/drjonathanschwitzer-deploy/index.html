Why There Is No Traffic Today

Plastic surgery falls under Google's Your Money or Your Life (YMYL) classification — content that directly impacts a person's health and safety. For these sites, Google applies its highest scrutiny through E-E-A-T signals: Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, and Trustworthiness. Meeting the minimum threshold isn't optional. It's the price of entry.

Dr. Schwitzer has everything Google wants — Ivy League credentials, board certification, a decade of surgical experience. The problem is that none of it is structured, signaled, or amplified in a way that Google or AI systems can read and reward.

The solution is not more pages or more keywords. It's building a digital authority ecosystem centered on Jonathan Schwitzer, M.D. as the defining expert in his market — where every piece of content, every backlink, every structured data signal points back to him as the credible, trusted answer for high-end plastic surgery in South Florida.

That ecosystem doesn't exist yet. That is why there is no traffic today.
